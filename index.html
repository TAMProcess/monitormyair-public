Dust — What's Really Floating in Your Air

The average home produces 40 lbs of dust per year. Every footstep, every HVAC cycle, every opened door sends skin cells, carpet fibers, insulation fragments, and fine particulates back into the air you breathe. The beam of light reveals particles that are always there but invisible to the naked eye.

How Mold Spores Hijack Your Dust

Mold spores don't just float alone — they seek out and bind to dust particles. Watch the green spores drift in and attach to ordinary dust. This is exactly how mycotoxins spread through your HVAC system into every room, onto every surface, and into your lungs.

VOCs — The Invisible Gas No Filter Catches

Candles, cleaning products, new carpet, and fresh paint release volatile organic compounds — invisible chemical gases that pass straight through standard HVAC filters. Your air can look perfectly clean while you're breathing formaldehyde, benzene, and toluene every single day.

Your home likely has a combination of all three. Want to know exactly what's in your air?
