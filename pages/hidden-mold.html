Hidden Mold

You Can't Always See It — But You Might Be Breathing It

Not all mold grows where you can see it. Some of the most significant mold problems in homes and buildings are completely invisible from the living space. Hidden mold grows behind walls, under floors, above ceiling tiles, inside wall cavities, and within HVAC systems — anywhere there's moisture and organic material.

  • Behind drywall — Especially on the back side (paper facing) of drywall that got wet from a leak
  • Under flooring — Beneath tile, laminate, or carpet where moisture was trapped
  • Inside wall cavities — Where condensation, plumbing leaks, or bulk water entered
  • Above ceiling tiles — From roof leaks or condensation from HVAC ductwork above
  • Inside cabinetry — Under sinks with slow leaks, or behind kitchen/bathroom cabinets against exterior walls
  • In HVAC systems — On coils, in drain pans, inside ductwork
  • In crawlspaces and attics — On framing, insulation, and sheathing
  • A persistent musty smell with no visible source
  • Unexplained health symptoms that improve when you leave the building
  • History of water damage, flooding, or leaks — even years ago
  • Discoloration or bubbling on walls or ceilings (may indicate moisture behind the surface)
  • High indoor humidity readings despite climate control
  • Condensation on windows or cold surfaces

If there's a smell and you can't see the source — something is growing where you can't see it.

Most mold inspections involve a visual walkthrough. But hidden mold requires more than looking at walls:

  • Moisture meters — Can detect elevated moisture behind surfaces without opening them
  • Thermal imaging — Shows temperature differentials that indicate moisture intrusion
  • Air sampling — Measures airborne spore levels to detect hidden reservoirs
  • Wall sampling — Small openings in strategic locations to directly observe cavity conditions

A good inspector uses multiple tools and investigates based on building history — not just what's visible.

  • Previous water damage that was "dried out" — Fans and dehumidifiers may have dried the surface but left moisture in cavities, leading to hidden mold growth weeks or months later.
  • Slow plumbing leak under a sink — Cabinet backs and wall cavities behind plumbing often have some of the worst hidden mold.
  • Roof leak that was "fixed" — The roof may be repaired, but the drywall and insulation that got wet were never addressed.
  • Ice dam damage — Water intrusion from ice dams can enter wall cavities from above, growing mold on framing that's completely concealed.
  1. Don't tear open walls yourself — You can spread spores and make the problem worse without proper containment.
  2. Document what you're experiencing — Symptoms, smells, moisture observations, water history.
  3. Hire a qualified inspector — One who does moisture mapping, not just visual inspection. Look for moisture meter, thermal imaging, and air sampling capabilities.
  4. Separate inspection from remediation — The person who finds the problem should not be the person who profits from fixing it.
