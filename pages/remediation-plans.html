Mold Remediation Plans

Why Most Mold Jobs Go Wrong — and What Protects You

A remediation plan is the written scope of work that defines what will be done, how it will be done, and how success will be measured. Without one, you're paying someone to do something with no agreed-upon standard for what "done" means. Most remediation disputes we see start here.

Most mold remediation disputes happen because there was no written plan — or the plan was vague. A proper remediation plan:

  • Defines the scope of work (what areas, what materials)
  • Specifies containment and worker protection requirements
  • Details how affected materials will be removed and disposed of
  • Describes how the moisture source will be addressed
  • Establishes clearance criteria — how you'll know the job is done

If a contractor says "we'll take care of it" without providing a written plan — that's a red flag.

A proper remediation plan has 7 critical components. Most plans we review are missing at least 2 or 3 of them — and those gaps are where homeowners lose money or end up with the same problem months later.

The biggest issues we see: vague scope descriptions, no clearance criteria, and no plan for the moisture source. If your plan doesn't address the cause of the mold, the mold comes back.
